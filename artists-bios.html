Artist & Musician Bios

The Composer

Ted Nash

Ted Nash is a recipient of two GrammyÂ® Awards and has been nominated several times for his many musical works. His most significant work, Presidential Suite, was inspired by great political speeches of the 20th century dealing with the theme of freedom. Nash transcribed the speeches for their actual musical pitches and created themes, placing them into contexts that embraced the speakers and the location and era of the speeches. For the recording, each track is introduced by an excerpt from the speech that inspired it, read by significant figures from the world of entertainment, politics and sports, including actors Glenn Close and Sam Waterston; Ambassador Andrew Young; Senator Joe Lieberman; authors Deepak Chopra and Douglas Brinkley; diplomats William vanden Heuvel and David Miliband.

Nash also composed Portrait in Seven Shades, a suite that consists of seven movements, each inspired by a master of modern art who worked in the century around the apex of jazz: Chagall, Dali, Matisse, Monet, Picasso, Pollock and Van Gogh. It was released by Jazz at Lincoln Center and performed by the world-renowned Jazz at Lincoln Center Orchestra (JALC) with Wynton Marsalis. Nash has performed with JALC on alto & soprano saxophone, flute, and clarinet for 25 years.

Nash was born in Los Angeles into a musical family (his father, Dick Nash, and uncle, the late Ted Nash, were both well-known jazz and studio musicians). He has that uncanny ability to mix freedom with substance, blues with intellect, and risk-taking with clarity. He is a co-founder of the New York-based Jazz Composers Collective, a musician-run, non-profit innovative entity dedicated to presenting the original works of composers pushing the boundaries of their self-expression.

Nash has become one of the most significant jazz composers of the 21st century. His recordings have received wide critical acclaim, appearing on the "best-of" lists in the New York Times, the New Yorker, the Village Voice, the Los Angeles Times, and the Boston Globe; and have charted on Billboard.

Collaborating Artists

Nancy X. Valentine

Nancy X. Valentine is a Chinese American artist and cultural leader living and making a life in Otter Tail County, Minnesota. Nancy is a recipient of numerous LRAC and MSAB grants, a Springboard for the Arts Rural Regenerator Fellow and has been recognized nationally as 1 of 14 Rural Innovators by the Biden/Harris Administration. In addition to her artistry, Nancy serves as the Executive Director of the Kaddatz Galleries in downtown Fergus Falls.

Eric A. Johnson

Currently, Johnson is an adjunct instructor at Minnesota State Community and Technical College and Mayville State University. He continues to create new work from his art and printmaking studio (Big Oak Press) near his home in Hillsboro, ND. His next exhibit will be at the Rourke Art Museum and Gallery in Moorhead, in March 2026.

Senta Lauren

Senta Lauren is a visual artist whose work reflects the beauty and resilience of the everyday landscapes she calls home. She began painting in December 2020 during a period of personal and professional transition, discovering a creative path that brought clarity, renewal, and a deeper sense of purpose. With a background in military and corporate environments and experience as a doctoral student, Senta's artistic journey emerged unexpectedly, but quickly became central to her life and expression.

Inspired by porches, farmsteads, gardens, and small-town streets, Senta captures the lived energy of spaces that hold stories, pride, and connection. Her paintings highlight the quiet dignity and vibrant creativity found in rural and small-community settings, revealing the hope and strength woven through ordinary places.

Senta has exhibited her work in venues across the country and is recognized for her distinctive perspective and ability to connect with viewers through both nostalgia and fresh interpretation. Her process blends traditional techniques with modern influences, resulting in pieces that resonate with contemporary and classic art lovers alike.

Committed to community engagement, Senta participates in local art initiatives and workshops, fostering creativity among emerging artists. She continues to expand her practice through new collaborations and mediums as she develops her artistic voice.

Guest Musicians

Christopher Marion

Christopher Marion is a violinist, composer, and improviser whose work bridges classical technique with contemporary creativity. He attended Berklee College of Music and has created music for Apple, Google, Facebook, Lexus, and Disney, contributing to advertising campaigns and live presentations. Earlier in his career, he served as concertmaster for Don Jon under composer Judson Crane.

Christopher has studied with Christian Howes, Randy Sabien, and is currently training intensively with Billy Contreras. After nine years in Nashville, he now lives in rural Wisconsin, where the quiet landscape informs his sound. He also performs regularly for local communities, including nursing homes and individuals at the end of life.

Diane Townsend

Diane Townsend is a versatile cellist known for her expressive interpretations and collaborative spirit. A graduate of the Juilliard School, she has performed as a soloist and chamber musician across North America and Europe. Her repertoire spans from Baroque to contemporary works, with a particular passion for exploring new compositions and cross-genre collaborations.

Diane has been featured in numerous festivals including Tanglewood, Aspen, and the Banff Centre, and has worked with leading conductors and ensembles. She is also an active educator, maintaining a private studio and serving as faculty at several music programs. Her commitment to bringing classical music to diverse audiences has led to innovative programming and community engagement initiatives.
